Biology Research Paper Program

Led by Dr. Chan- Cancer Biology PhD from USC. An introductory course designed for students to develop the skills needed to read, analyze, and communicate primary research in life sciences. Students will explore the structure of scientific papers, assess the validity of findings, understand the peer review process, and present their understanding to a general audience.

A PhD in Cancer Biology and Genomics from USC, Dr. Priscilla Chan is a postdoctoral researcher advancing breakthrough treatments for glioblastoma, one of the most aggressive brain cancers. Her pioneering research on circadian clock compounds as novel cancer therapies has earned national recognition, including the Society for Research in Biological Rhythms Excellence Award and a prestigious NIH fellowship. With publications in top-tier journals and presentations at international conferences, she collaborates with leading universities and biopharma companies to bring cutting-edge treatments from lab to clinical trials. Dr. Chan's work bridges molecular biology and translational medicine, positioning her at the forefront of cancer research innovation while mentoring the next generation of scientists.

Week by Week Curriculum

Week 1: Introduction to Scientific Research & Primary Literature

  • Syllabus overview and introductions
  • Primary vs. secondary sources
  • Parts of a research paper
  • Introduction to PubMed

Week 2: Dissecting Abstract, Introduction & Research Questions

  • Guided breakdown of abstract
  • Guided breakdown of introduction
  • Identify research question and hypothesis

Week 3: Methods & Experimental Design

  • Discuss variables, controls, and sample size
  • Methodology overview
  • Diagram example experiment

Week 4: Analyzing Results & Discussion

  • Analyze sample figures
  • Group activity: match conclusions to visuals
  • Summary of findings
  • Identify limitations and future directions

Week 5: Research Ethics, Scientific Integrity & Critical Reading

  • Discussion of misconduct examples
  • Group debate on ethical scenarios
  • Critique of a short paper
  • Develop a quality checklist

Week 6: Communicating Science & Final Presentations

  • Compare scientific paper and related news article
  • Discussion on public communication
  • Introduction to BioRender
  • Presentation workshop

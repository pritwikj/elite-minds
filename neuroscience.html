Dr. Danielle Bolling

Dr. Danielle Bolling is a Yale-trained neuroscientist with over a decade of experience in brain research, teaching, and student mentorship. She holds a PhD in Neuroscience from Yale University, a Masters (with Distinction) from University College London, and a BA from Cornell University. Her research on social cognition and autism has led to over 18 peer-reviewed publications and invited presentations at leading neuroscience institutions, including her talk on "Social Cognition in Children with Autism Spectrum Disorders" at the Yale Magnetic Resonance Research Center. Previously the Academic Supervisor for Alpha Scholars, Dr. Bolling led curriculum development and trained Harvard student mentors for a global high school research program. She has served as an instructor at both Yale University and the University of Chicago. With a strong foundation in neuroscience and a passion for guiding young scholars, Dr. Bolling brings both academic excellence and personalized mentorship to this program.

Week by Week Curriculum

Week 1: Introduction to the human brain

  • Welcome, course goals, and class structure
  • Overview of human brain functions: thoughts, memory, emotions, senses, movement, language, and more
  • Why study the human brain? Group brainstorm and personal reflections

Week 2: Human brain anatomy

  • Brain structure: sections, hemispheres, lobes, and key regions
  • Neuroanatomical planes, directional labels, and navigation terminology
  • Blood supply: major arteries and the blood-brain barrier

Week 3: Brain cells

  • Types of brain cells: neurons and glia
  • How neurons communicate: action potentials, synapses, neurotransmitters
  • Overview of key neurotransmitters and neuronal plasticity

Week 4: Studying the brain

  • Scientific tools for studying the brain: EEG, MRI/fMRI, NIRS, MEG, PET/SPECT, MRS, TMS, etc.
  • Research study designs: longitudinal, cross-sectional, case-control, and more

Week 5: Final project: The Research Proposal

  • Purpose and format of research proposals
  • Steps for creating your proposal: topic selection, background research, question/hypothesis generation, and methodology
  • Group brainstorm and feedback on topic selection

Week 6: Background research & research question development

  • How to conduct background research and identify credible sources
  • Writing a literature review and creating a reference list
  • Developing strong, focused research questions

Week 7: Developing the hypothesis & study methodology

  • Choosing neuroscience tools and experimental methods for your research question
  • Role of hypothesis-testing and example methodologies
  • Peer feedback and preparing your research proposal slide deck

Week 8: Final project presentations

  • Individual research proposal presentations
  • Discussion on next steps in research: publication, high school and college research
  • Group course reflection

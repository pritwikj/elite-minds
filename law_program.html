Pre-Law Research Passion Project

Students create a compelling, university-level legal research memo that makes their college application stand out. In this immersive program, they will analyze real-world cases, practice oral arguments in a mock hearing, and master advanced legal reasoning under the guidance of a Princeton scholar. The final memo serves as a powerful demonstration of the academic rigor and intellectual curiosity that top universities seek.

Group Size 10 students
Outcome
  • Draft a ~2-page Legal Research Memo
  • Earn an Intro to Law Certificate of Completion
  • Understand key legal terms, institutions, and processes
  • Write a clear, structured case brief and short legal memo
  • Experience a mock hearing and practice oral argument

Week by Week Curriculum

Week 1: Introduction

  • Discover legal careers and practice areas
  • Overview of how the U.S. legal system works
  • Choose an area of interest and research sources

Week 2: Sources of the Law

  • Where laws come from: statutes and common law
  • Civil vs. criminal law—real-world examples
  • Select memo topic and begin background research

Week 3: The Judicial System

  • How courts are structured and work together
  • Evaluate your chosen law: purpose, who it affects, enforcement
  • Continue targeted research to build evidence

Week 4: Trial Mechanics

  • How trials work: juries, verdicts, and famous cases
  • Practice close reading of cases
  • Begin structured analysis of your law

Week 5: Business Organizations

  • Business entities 101 and why they matter
  • Wrap analysis; consider improvements and community impact
  • Compare to similar laws to find best practices

Week 6: Interpreting the Law

  • How lawyers interpret laws; read a Supreme Court excerpt
  • Finalize proposals and draft the ~2‑page memo
  • Present findings; practice oral argument skills

Legal Research Memo

Goals

  • Identify legal areas of interest
  • Engage in legal research and analysis to evaluate a law or judicial decision
  • Convey research and analysis with clear, effective writing
  • Identify strengths and weaknesses of proposed changes to the law or decision

Memo Components

  • Introduction and Legal Excerpt
  • Research and Legal Background
  • Legal Analysis
  • Improvement Proposals
  • Conclusion

Berkeley Astrophysics Blueprint: Space Research Lab

Conduct a certified astrophysics research project alongside a UC Berkeley astrophysicist. Students will learn to analyze astronomical data, conduct original research, and complete a mentored project in cutting-edge topics like exoplanets and dark matter.

Taught By

Isabella Deutsch

Isabella Deutsch, a distinguished researcher at UC Berkeley Physics, leads cutting-edge investigations at the Berkeley Lab ATLAS Experiment. Her prestigious research fellowship at CIERA Northwestern University advanced breakthrough studies in supernova analysis and particle physics. A member of the US ATLAS Summer Undergraduate Program for Exceptional Researchers, Isabella has been through the most elite research programs in the field. Now working alongside Professor Shapiro's renowned research team, Isabella continues to push boundaries in high-energy particle physics while mentoring the next generation of astrophysicists.

Week by Week Curriculum

Week 1: Introduction to the Vastness of Astrophysics

  • Program overview and expectations
  • Fundamentals of astrophysics and required skill sets
  • Deep dive into celestial objects: comets, meteors, planets, galaxies
  • Introduction to cosmology and universe expansion

Week 2: Supernovae & Exoplanets

  • Time domain astronomy and supernovae mechanics
  • Interactive supernova simulation activities
  • Exoplanet formation and detection methods
  • Understanding habitable zones

Week 3: Observational Astronomy

  • Telescope types and data collection methods
  • Optical vs infrared astronomy
  • Kepler's Laws and orbital mechanics
  • Hands-on telescope simulation exercises

Week 4: Project Development & Research Methods

  • Project selection and planning
  • Research methodology overview
  • Source evaluation and documentation techniques
  • Scientific presentation and communication skills

Week 5: Collaborative Research

  • Group research coordination
  • Resource consolidation and management
  • Presentation role assignment
  • Guided project work sessions

Week 6: Presentation Development

  • Outline development and review
  • Structured presentation building
  • Mentor consultation sessions
  • Progress evaluation and feedback

Week 7: Peer Review & Final Preparation

  • Structured peer feedback sessions
  • Presentation refinement and technical checks
  • Group collaboration workshops
  • Final mentor review and rehearsals

Week 8: Final Presentations

  • Student research presentations
  • Expert feedback and evaluation
  • Future research pathway discussion
  • Program completion celebration
